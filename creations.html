Lady at the Diner

"Lady at the diner" is inspired by Alice Neel's portraits. I first encountered Alice Neel at Sara Kay Gallery, New York, in 2019. Her portraits, or "Pictures of People" as she preferred, was up next to Picasso's work. I was immediately drawn to the way she painted Lida Moser, her photojournalist friend. Neel's paintings captured the individuality of her subject but also reflected her perception of them.
Alice Neel (1900 - 1984), an American artist, is famously known for her portraits of her friends, family and other contemporary artists of her time. She had a unique style of using color to express the subject‚Äôs emotions. She never wavered from her painting style, which was marked by social realism, and never hesitated to show people when they're at their most vulnerable - claiming herself to be a "Collector of Souls". Her other paintings included still life and cityscapes.

The Masked Man

The Masked Man" is inspired by Margaret Keane paintings. I was drawn to Keane's paintings because of the subjects' eyes. Back when I had just started drawing regularly, I would skip drawing eyes - worried that I would mess it up.
Margaret Keane McGuire, an American artist, is known for her big-eyed paintings. She started painting at a young age. One striking feature of all her paintings is the "big-eyed" subject.
After moving to San Francisco, she met a real estate agent named Walter Keane who she married shortly after. Margaret's paintings were available in art exhibitions. Post marriage, Walter, with his charm, took care of marketing and sales. Soon, Keane's paintings, prints, postcards and posters were seen almost in every household. These paintings were signed "Keane". But who was the real Keane painting these?
Margaret didn't realise at first that her husband was taking credit for all her paintings. She was expected to paint throughout the day while Walter sold these and earned big sums of money. "I was always drawing eyes, even as a child. Eyes fascinated me."
Her big-eyed paintings soon reflected the sadness and anguish she was suffering in her second marriage. Being unable to call her paintings as her own, tormented her. Soon after their divorce, Margaret sued her husband and claimed that the paintings were hers. After a long legal battle and "paint off" challenging Walter, both the Keane's were asked to paint in front of the jury. She won the case.
"Big Eyes" (2014) starring Amy Adams & Christoph Waltz is based on Margaret's life.

Portrait of a Woman

"Don't judge a book by its cover" but it was Ella Fitzgerald's album cover with Bernard Buffet's artwork that caught my attention.
Bernard Buffet (1928-1999) is a French expressionist painter. His works largely included portraits, still-lifes and lithographs. While abstract art was gaining popularity, Buffet continued to vouch for representational art. His work prominently expressed the postwar period. His painting style with "spiky, angular and elongated forms" is easily distinguishable from his contemporaries.
