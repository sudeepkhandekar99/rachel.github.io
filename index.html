Lady at the diner

"Lady at the diner" is inspired by Alice Neel's portraits.I first encountered Alice Neel at @sarakaygallery in 2019. Her portraits, or "Pictures of People" as she preferred, was up next to Picasso's work. I was immediately drawn to the way she painted Lida Moser, her photojournalist friend. Neel's paintings captured the individuality of her subject but also reflected her perception of them. She never wavered from her painting style and never hesitated to show people when they're at their most vulnerable - claiming herself to be a "Collector of Souls". Her other paintings included still life and cityscapes.
